About Myself

Currently heading into my senior year of architecture school, I study at The Catholic University of America, in D.C., where I am enrolled in the IPAL (Integrated Path to Architectural Licensure) program. Part of the IPAL curriculum requires that students work three separate eight month long internships, the first of which begins May-June 2019 and ends late December 2019. My interest in architecture sparked back in 7th grade when I began 3d modeling using Solidworks. In high school I helped found a FRC robotics team with a focus on design and manufacturing. From robotics, I gained many practical skills including leadership roles, 3d modeling, metal manufacturing, mig/tig welding, electrical wiring, and assembly tasks. I am interested in architecture because it combines my past experience in design with my natural knack of creativity and art.
